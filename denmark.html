Denmark

Denmark is one of the European Union's most vigorous members in recruiting accomplished personnel around the world. The nation has been ranked the topmost country in terms of income equality, the happiest and the second most peaceful place in the world.

Denmark with an attractive corporate culture, flourishing IT industry, and laid back European lifestyle, Denmark turns out to be a very good prospect for someone who wants to live and work abroad. Denmark allows skilled individuals to attain a residence permit visa based exclusively on their credentials for the reason of finding work in Denmark. The residence permit is approved subsequent to a successful evaluation based on a point based system, which scores applicants on factors such as age, educational level, language skills, and work experience.

There are plentiful reasons which make Denmark the most preferred country to migrate these days. The most fundamental reason is that the Denmark immigration process is uncomplicated unlike other countries including USA, UK, Canada, Australia etc. The country's immigration policy is flexible and invites people from all over the world to get a residence and work permit.

The complimentary weather of the country suits the European and the Asian denizens in equal parts. The ample ecology and diversified natural features make it very lively in terms of the beauty and appeal. The country proves excellence in education sector too, which is the reason why tens of thousands of students are enrolled in the country which provides them all the necessary training to strive in the globalized economy.

The Positive List: There are many professions in Denmark which are deficient of qualified workers. If you have skills in one of these occupations, you can acquire a work and residence permit in exceptionally less time under the Positive List scheme.

The Pay Limit Scheme: If you have a job offer from a Danish employer that pays more than DKK 375,000 (approximately USD 55,000), you are also eligible for a work and residence permit in Denmark under the Pay Limit scheme.

Eligibility Necessities

In order to establish oneself with a residence permit under the green card scheme, one must manage to achieve a minimum of 100 points. These points are decided on the basis of educational level, language skills, work experience, adaptability, and age.

Important Information for Denmark Green Card Holders

Persons shifting to Denmark should have an official address within six months of getting their Denmark green card. It is the accountability of the person opting for Denmark immigration to locate a place to live.

It is a necessity that Denmark green card holder takes out a full health insurance covering the period until you are allowed to be covered by the Danish National Health Insurance.

It is also a major necessity that in the first year after being granted a residence permit the person opting for Denmark immigration earns a minimum of DKK 50,000. This requirement will be routinely checked by the Danish Agency for International Recruitment and Integration and in case of it not being fulfilled, the application of the person opting for Denmark immigration may be revoked or his extension denied.

Denmark Green card holders do not require a work permit. The residence permit gives them the right to carry out paid or unpaid work. However, it does not give persons opting for immigration to Denmark the right to work as a self-employed person.
